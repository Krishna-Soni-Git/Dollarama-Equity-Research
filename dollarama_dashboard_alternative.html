Core Thesis — BUY · Target $212 · ~25% Upside

On March 24, 2026, Dollarama reported FY2026 results and the stock fell 9%. Revenue grew 13%. EPS grew 13%. The selloff was driven by a margin dip from the Reject Shop acquisition in Australia. We view this as a temporary integration headwind, not a structural impairment to the business model. ROIC of ~30% vs WACC of 9% — a 21-point spread — has widened for five consecutive years. The market is pricing permanent damage. We think it is pricing a headwind.

Critical AI judgment — why we discarded this as a valuation tool
The high R² is misleading. MA is derived from price itself, creating near-perfect autocorrelation. The model predicts price using a lagged version of price — not discovering causal economic relationships. This is a classic data leakage pattern in financial ML. We label it Tutorial 5 output and explicitly exclude it from our DCF-based recommendation. This is exactly the kind of AI limitation that Section 2.C requires us to identify.

09  ·  Section 2.C  ·  ~1:00
AI Critical Evaluation
What we accepted, what we rejected, and why — professional judgment over automation
~1:00 min

AI Integration Principle

AI compressed weeks of data gathering into hours. But every number is traceable to a source, formula, and human decision. AI accelerates research. It does not replace judgment. We are accountable for every metric in this analysis.

✓ ACCEPTED
EBITDA margin trend detection
AI correctly identified 5-year margin expansion in every year. Confirmed against Dollarama IR annual reports — retained as a core investment thesis pillar. This was the primary output that strengthened our quality argument.
✗ DISCARDED
ROIC using equity denominator
AI used NOPAT/Equity — invalid. Dollarama had negative book equity in FY2022 because buybacks exceeded retained earnings. Replaced with EBIT/(Assets−CL−Cash). Most critical correction in the entire analysis.
✓ ACCEPTED
Monte Carlo + NLP sentiment
Monte Carlo: 5,000 paths, triangular distribution for FCF growth, normal for WACC — validated and kept. NLP polarity on 5 real earnings call transcripts: accepted as supplementary corroboration of bullish management tone. Neither is a primary valuation driver.
✗ DISCARDED
Altman Z-Score as distress signal
AI generated Z > 10 (“Safe Zone”). Discarded: calibrated on 1960s US manufacturers — inapplicable to a Canadian IFRS 16 discount retailer. Score reflects asset turnover efficiency, not solvency.
The principle: AI made us faster. It did not make us right.
Speed vs. judgment. Pattern recognition vs. economic reasoning. Automation vs. accountability. AI cannot distinguish a goodwill impairment from an operating loss. It cannot apply the correct ROIC formula for a negative-equity retailer. It cannot know the Altman Z-Score is inapplicable here. That judgment — grounded in accounting discipline and business context — stayed with us throughout.

Three reasons to own it
ROIC far exceeds WACC — every year, widening
~30% ROIC vs 9% WACC = ~21pp spread. Widened for 5 consecutive years. Every dollar of capital deployed earns roughly three times its cost. This is the single most important metric in the analysis — it is what justifies the premium multiple.
Growth runway is long, visible, and underpriced
584 more Canadian stores to 2,200 target. Dollarcity at 632 stores growing 14%. Australia 700-store target not yet modelled. Three growth engines at once.
Recession-resilient, countercyclical demand
Fixed-price discount benefits from consumer down-trading in inflation environments. Dollarama gains traffic when the economy weakens — structural tailwind in current macro environment.
Three things that would change our rating
Reject Shop drag extends beyond 2027
If Australian margin dilution persists past 2027, consolidated EBITDA stays compressed and our DCF is too optimistic. We would revisit the target.
China tariff shock to COGS
The $5.00 price cap limits Dollarama's ability to pass on input cost increases. A sustained COGS spike compresses margins directly and would trigger a rating review.
SSS drops and stays below 2%
Comparable store sales of 4.9% in FY2025 Q4 is healthy. A sustained decline below 2% signals genuine demand erosion — the only fundamental scenario that would make us sell.
